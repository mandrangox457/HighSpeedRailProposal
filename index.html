2. The New York HSR Project & Core Benefits

This initiative proposes a new, dedicated High-Speed Rail (HSR) service on the **Empire Corridor** (New York City to Albany/Buffalo). We will use modern electric trains and infrastructure upgrades to achieve competitive travel times, offering a superior alternative to congested highways and short-haul flights.

Key Public and Economic Benefits

  • Exceptional Economic Return: For every $1 invested in HSR, approximately **four dollars in broader economic benefits** are generated.
  • Climate Action: HSR produces **83% fewer greenhouse gas emissions** than driving, making it a critical tool for achieving state climate goals and reducing air pollution.
  • Congestion Relief: Directly addresses the severe congestion on Interstate 87. U.S. urban congestion cost **$179 billion in 2017** due to 8.8 billion hours of delay and wasted fuel.
  • Economic Integration: Critically links and integrates the Upstate and Downstate New York economies.

Implementation at a Glance (Six-Step Plan)

The project is structured in two main construction phases to expedite implementation in the highest-priority section (NYP to Albany).

  1. Phase 1 Construction (NYP to Albany): Builds the high-priority section, including new right-of-ways to eliminate sharp turns, alongside the initial order of new electric trains.
  2. Phase 2 Construction (Albany to Buffalo): Begins while Phase 1 is in testing. This phase requires additional track upgrades and further train set orders to match ridership growth.
  3. Rigorous Testing: Both phases require non-passenger operation testing to ensure safety and performance before opening for public service.
